Symbiogenesis Season 6 Item Check List

World Tree Talisman -True- A talisman created around the theme of law. Depicted upon it is the World Tree, with its beautiful pine needles, and a dragon at the center that radiates authority.
Incomplete Generator (CH6) An uncharged generator, dormant and non-functional. Those who possess it can envision a new world.
Shinobi Grappling Hook A shinobi tool used to climb walls and trees, custom-made by Tetsubishi Heavy Industries. It allows one to instantly scale obstacles that are up to 30 meters in height.
Anti-Viral Hose A device that disperses a vaccine against the virus. A product of the efforts of ancient engineers, it is a symbol of hope to all.
Gold Sword -King- A sword given as an offering to the Twelve Blademasters of the Government by a master craftsman during the Six Powers Era. Its gorgeous form is fit for a king.
Modified Part: Crazy Generator An extremely powerful energy generator created by a Crazy Doc for Guards. The average Guard would explode with it installed due to how hard it is to control.
Undersecretary's Electromagnetic Knife An electromagnetic survival knife used lovingly by a hero of old. From the edge alone, you can sense the struggles of its previous owners as they traversed the wilderness.
Hidden Stronghold Permit A permit held only by a select group of Merchants that have earned the recognition of the Chief. It reminds one of the Black Marketeers, due to its jet black color, and has a good weight to it.
Metal Parts Detector An advanced metal detector that reacts to parts inside bodies. Nothing can escape its sight, making it an effective tool against Guard shinobi.
Large Dragonite Crystal A gemstone found in the Great Tree Labyrinth. Legends say it contains the power of an ancient god, but it may be just that—a legend.
Electric Jitte A self-defense device that uses a powerful electric current to knock opponents unconscious. It makes a loud crackling noise when activated, and is a favorite among shinobis and watchmen who use lightning arts.
Fire Arts Shinobi Tool: Rageflame Orb One of the shinobi tools favored by those who practice the fire arts. Causes a fire to break out across a wide area when thrown—an inferno that will consume all.
Government Ruins Data Storage An ancient artifact discovered at the Government ruins. Specific details about it are unclear, but one theory proposes that the owner worked for the leader of the researchers.
Hero's Guide to Warfare A guide to warfare passed down among Guards since ancient times. It contains the tactics and maxims of a battalion commander from a bygone age. It covers a wide range of topics, including the latest shinobi arts of the time.
Forbidden Scroll: Thousand Thunders A forbidden scroll that contains an advanced Lightning Style technique: Thousand Thunders. Due to the strain it causes, the ability has been banned.
Doc-Style Dice Cup A device used for cheating, created by Crazy Doc students with too much time on their hands. By applying a faint magnetic force, you can manipulate the sides of the dice inside.
Dice Table A gambling table that is likely being used to play Dice somewhere. The regional rules are engraved into its solid wooden surface.
Innocent Sculpture A stone sculpture of what appears to be a mother and child. It has an indescribable eeriness about it, yet you can feel the maker's deep love for their creation as you hold it in your hands.
Old Photo of the Frontier Vehicle An old photograph of the Frontier Vehicle, which once ventured across Terrestria, and its caravan crew. People who appear to be representatives of the Normies and Spikes are standing with their arms around each other's shoulders.
Compass for the Great Tree Labyrinth A compass that reacts to the mysterious power emitted by the Dragonite. It is considered by treasure hunters to be an essential item for those who wish to challenge the Great Tree Labyrinth.
Wolf Meat with Penal Colony Persimmon Sauce A local dish from the Penal Colony—created by a chef from Inu Village. It is made from a variety of regional ingredients, making it a true taste of the camp.
Zodiac Promise Cup A cup that was exchanged when swearing in the Undersecretary's twelve disciples. Made by a famous blacksmith, the pottery is also of great historical value.
Cursed Sword: Muramasa A blade that was always with its owner—a legendary assassin—until the end. Rumor has it that it seeks new blood every night—a thirst brought on by its bottomless hatred.
Prototype: Mechanical Brain An artificial brain being developed by Tetsubishi Heavy Industries that is one step away from practical application. Research is progressing well, and they say the engineers behind it have had their brains replaced with mechanical versions.
Dragon Tarot Case A case for storing tarot cards. From the intricate depictions of dragons and people upon it, you can sense the depth of the Draconic Faith's history and culture.
Code-Breaking Manual A handbook on code breaking. To ensure secrecy, the text itself is scrambled. You will need to collect different volumes and refer to each of them to decipher the contents.
Trash Mound Gas Mask A life-saving mask that offers protection against the gases emitted from garbage. Not having one on can have serious effects on health, shortening one's lifespan. Sadly, most of the residents of the Trash Mound just don't have the money to spare.
Guard Neurotoxin A neurotoxin that works on Guards, and has seen long use in battles between shinobis. It is equally effective against mechanical parts and flesh. Given how potent it is, even small amounts are lethal.
Paradise Pill (High Quality) A smart drug that fetches high prices at the Penal Colony. A popular choice for Guard fighters before their matches, and doctors before they begin complex operations to modify parts.
Guard Neuron Part A versatile part used to grow a variety of Guard organs. Doctors of skill are able to utilize these efficiently.
Dragonite Gem A raw gemstone cluster found deep within the Great Tree Labyrinth. Said to hold enough power to craft a single barrier device, its value is immeasurable. Sought desperately by both Soulkin and merchants alike.
Dragonite Shard A gemstone fragment found in the Great Tree Labyrinth. The abundance of impurities within means it cannot be used for jewelry. However, it is still popular as a souvenir due to its rarity.
Raven Trap Glue A powerful adhesive, made to throw at giant crows for extermination purposes. Explodes as it flies through the air, spreading sticky glue all around.
Tatsu Clan Smokebomb Smoke bombs used by the Guards of the Tatsu Clan. Made with compounds that induce hallucinations of dragons, causing anyone who inhales it to be frozen in fear.
Master Craftsman's Flame Kunai A kunai of deep crimson, forged by a skilled craftsman. A small heating implement is installed within, letting it reach temperatures that can burn anything that stands against it.
Resonating Bell A portable bell that's taken into the Great Tree Labyrinth by groups of adventurers. While small, it echoes well, making it useful for avoiding Giant Lifeforms and locating your allies.
Secret Village Pills Pills made in the Shinobi Village using a method passed down through the generations. They are said to have all sorts of effects, and can be used to enhance health, treat poisoning, stop pain, and relieve hangovers.
World Tree Oil Natural resin harvested from the trunk of the World Tree. In addition to being a fuel and a source of energy, it has been shown to relax the body when consumed.
Penal Colony Sake A refreshing sake with a sharp spiciness that goes straight to your nose. It is often served at celebrations or before decisive battles.
Shinobi Yo-Yo A new weapon created by young shinobis. While it looks harmless, it packs a big punch—and from a distance, too. Many novice shinobi end up seriously hurting themselves when practicing to use one.
Courtesan's Iron Fan An iron fan that courtesans hide on their person for self-defense. It can be used to hit attackers when closed, and thrown in their face when open.
Wind Arts Shinobi Tool: Whirlwind Shuriken A projectile used by shinobis. It is fitted with a small, advanced gyro device, which allows its trajectory to be controlled when in flight.
Dung Bomb A foul-smelling ball that causes noses to turn. Scatters animal feces when thrown, creating a hellish scene for those around.
Polluted Amber A piece of amber with something dark trapped inside. Apparently, something stirs in the gloom sometimes... It was imported for researchers on the Floating Continent.
Penal Colony Sweet Persimmon A famous fruit from Saru Village. Legend goes that a lake crab brought a seed to land, and it grew into the first persimmon tree. There isn't any scientific basis for this anecdote, though. On rare occasions, a sweet persimmon is found — and when it is, it's highly prized.
Penal Colony Sour Persimmon A famous fruit from Saru Village. Legend goes that a lake crab brought a seed to land, and it grew into the first persimmon tree. There isn't any scientific basis for this anecdote, though.
Soluna Cake A famous rice cake that is popular in the Penal Colony's castle town. Enjoying one while watching the harvest moon is a seasonal tradition there.
Giant Electric Catfish Whisker Whiskers of an electric catfish that generates a high voltage and current. Among novice shinobi at the Penal Colony, getting shocked when trying to harvest one is a common tradition.
Container Key A key that opens the containers that travel to Post-Vegas via the Circle Railway. They're a common sight across the continent—a fact that shows how incompetent the management is.
Tetsubishi Welding Rig A welding rig used by employees of Tetsubishi Heavy Industries. Guards can withstand a fair amount of heat, but apply this implement and their bodies will warp in an instant.
Mod Doctor Auxiliary Arm Designed for busy doctors who need all the help they can get. It can handle all sorts of tasks—from taking out surgical tools to sewing up incisions.
Downtown Pamphlet A brochure that introduces the bustling downtown area near the Penal Colony's castle town. It depicts a place full of entertainment and pleasure. Tourists visiting for the first time are sure to be overwhelmed.
Downtown Shisha Shisha is popular in the Penal Colony, especially in downtown areas. It has become a new alternative to cigars, pipes, and energy herbs. It is especially popular among Merchants.
Dragonite Rosary Prayer beads with an eye-catching deep purple color. For the Undead living in the Penal Colony, products made from Dragonite are highly prized.
Mythril Knife A knife made of Dragonite. While it was created for aesthetic reasons, it has a keen edge and is robust enough to be practical.
Fossilized Bindweed A fossilized sample of bindweed that was grown by a faction of the civilization of the past. It is believed that the effects of Deadly Pollution turned the plant into stone.
Fossilized Lily A fossilized sample of a lily that was grown by a faction of the civilization of the past. Following a careful decontamination process, large numbers of these lilies were brought to the Floating Continent and became objects of study.
Undersecretary's Biography A biography that recounts the heroic tale of the Undersecretary who single-handedly saved Inu Village by defeating the giant wolf leader. Stories of his feats are still passed down by the people there.
Tatsu Seal Case A seal case given to the Tatsu swordsmen of the Lunarian combat brigade. It allows its bearer to wield the might of the party with a single stamp.
Marbled Giant Wolf Meat Marbled meat from a giant wolf. It has a unique smell and a chewy texture—characteristics that often make it a favorite. In the hands of a skilled chef, it can be made into something extremely special.
Cliff Garlic A species of garlic that has evolved uniquely on the Floating Continent. Delicious whether boiled or grilled, it also helps relieve fatigue—an effect that makes it much loved by shinobis.
Penal Colony Rice Rice that is grown on farmland near the Penal Colony's castle town. As the quality of the land and environment are poor, it is inferior to Third Colony Rice.
Shinobi Village Pickles Cliff vegetables that have been preserved in rice bran from the Shinobi Village. The perfect thing to replenish the minerals lost by the body during clan requests and training.
Rare Terrestrian Metal A precious ore brought in from Terrestria through the Grand Portal in Post Vegas. It is an essential material for the manufacture of crucial parts.
Tifanes Ruby A shining crimson ruby cut by Tifanes & Co. The company may have found a new mine recently, as they are currently being produced in large numbers.
Mod Doctor Surgery Kit Contains tools used by Guard Mod Doctors. Includes scalpels, screwdrivers, surgical implements, spare parts, painkillers—basically everything needed for modification surgery.
Prototype: Enhanced Blood A newly-invented blood substitute. Imbued with World Tree Energy, researchers hope it will allow Guards to make the most of their abilities.
Dragon Tarot Card -Birth- A tarot card linked to the Draconic Faith. It depicts a dragon beating its wings with fervor as people gaze on in delight. It appears to signal the arrival of a new era.
